How to Stop Procrastinating

Procrastination is not laziness. It is usually caused by overwhelm, fear of failure, or not knowing where to start. The goal is not motivation — the goal is making the first step so small that you cannot avoid it.

Why You Keep Procrastinating

Most people procrastinate because the task feels too big or unclear. Your brain avoids discomfort and chooses easier activities instead.

The solution is not to try harder, but to reduce the size of the task until it feels easy to start.

The 5-Minute Rule

Tell yourself: “I will only do this for 5 minutes.” Once you start, your brain usually continues automatically because starting is the hardest part.

Break Everything Into Tiny Steps

Instead of “study math”, your first step should be: open the book → find one page → read one example.

Remove Decision Pressure

Don’t ask “How do I finish everything?” Ask “What is the next physical action I can take right now?”

Use Instant Action

If you are stuck and don’t know where to start, Instant Action turns your stress into one simple next step.
